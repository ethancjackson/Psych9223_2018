Tutorial 2 - General Linear Model


The general linear model (GLM) is a fundamental statistical tool for regression analysis that is widely used for fMRI data. In this tutorial, you will learn the basics of univariate BOLD timecourse modelling using the GLM. First, we will explore how GLM analysis works one voxel at a time. Then we will move to BrainVoyager to do the analysis over an entire volume.

In fMRI data analysis, we are often interested in assessing how well recorded BOLD signal fits the expected response from our experimental design. The GLM allows a linear combination of predictors to be optimally fit to a timecourse. Let's consider a simple example using one predictor.

Recall that the GLM is used to model data using the form y = β0 + β1X1 + β2X2 + ... + βnXn + e where y is the observed or recorded signal, <β0 is a constant or intercept adjustment, β1 ... βn are the beta weights or scaling factors, and X1 ... Xn represent the expected haemodynamic response, according to the design matrix.
